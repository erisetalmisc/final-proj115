The History of (the) Laws

The story of my family, not legal studies

Aleris Law

Growing up a third culture kid, I’ve recently been interested in family history, and thought this project would be a good opportunity to learn more about my ancestors. Unlike many Western families, I am unable to trace my lineage back farther than a couple generations, and we have no records of anyone farther back than my great-grandparents. This can be attributed to lack of education; my ancestors were either farmers or fishermen.

I first decided to focus on the travels of my family as they immigrated in search of better lives, as they are the reason I am standing in the United States today.

Having learned more about our geographical roots, I wondered why our last name was “Law”, when our origins are Chinese. Upon asking my father, he explained what had happened to its pronunciation.

Afterwards, I took the time to search through old photos of our family. We don’t have a lot here in the U.S.; it’s mostly pictures of my parents while they were dating or my sister and I as children. We don’t have a photo of both sides of the family together at the same time, and it’s rare we get to take a photo with every member of even one side.
